The Sovereignty of Self:
The Awakening in New England

Transcendentalism, born in the 1830s amid the intellectual heart of New England, teaches that truth and spiritual awakening are obtained through intuition and self-trust. It is a bold rejection of the "cold" intellectualism of Unitarianism, favoring instead a direct, vibrant spiritual experience.

“Whoso would be a man must be a nonconformist.” — Ralph Waldo Emerson

Inspired by European Romanticism, this movement seeks to awaken the "Over-soul"—that spiritual essence connecting all living beings.
